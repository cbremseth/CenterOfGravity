Geograpic Midpoint Calculator

What is this?

Enter some points and This tool helps you find the midpoint or geometric median of multiple geographical points. Whether you're trying to find the most centralized location for a new store, plotting emergency service locations, or simply curious about geographical data, this tool offers a simple way to analyze points on a map.

How do I use it?

  1. Adding Points: You have two ways to add points for analysis.
    • Click on the Map: Simply click anywhere on the map to add a point. A marker will appear on the map, and its coordinates will be listed below.
    • Manually Enter Coordinates: Use the provided input fields to manually enter the latitude and longitude of a point, then click the "Add" button.
  2. Deleting points: You have two ways to remove existing points.
    • From the Map: Click on a marker to remove it from the map and the list.
    • From the List: Use the "Delete" button next to the coordinates in the list to remove both the marker and the entry.
  3. Calculate: Once you've added your points, click the "Calculate" button to find the midpoint or geometric median, depending on the number of points you've entered. The result will be displayed both as a marker on the map and as coordinates below the button.
  4. Reset: To start over, click the "Reset" button. This will remove all markers and coordinate entries.

How does it work?

  • Midpoint Calculation: When you have exactly two points, the tool calculates the midpoint, which is simply the average of the latitude and longitude coordinates of the two points.
  • Geometric Median: For three or more points, the tool calculates the geometric median. The geometric median minimizes the sum of distances to all other points. This calculation uses an iterative algorithm to converge towards the most centralized point.
